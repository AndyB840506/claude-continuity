For BTQ EP.16 ("The Wall" — Pink Floyd / Roger Waters), the user chose a **prism splitting light into a rainbow** as the central visual motif for cover art — this is the iconic *Dark Side of the Moon* album art, not *The Wall*'s imagery.

**Why:** The user's reasoning — using the prism gives the audience instant visual context that the episode is about Pink Floyd, even though the actual album/theme discussed is *The Wall*. Recognizability of the artist trumps literal album-cover accuracy.

**How to apply:** When generating cover-art prompts for BTQ episodes about a specific album/work by a well-known artist, consider whether a more universally recognizable visual signature of that artist (even from a different album) communicates "who this episode is about" faster than a literal depiction of the actual subject matter.
